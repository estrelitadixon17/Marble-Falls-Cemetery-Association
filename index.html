About Our Association

The Marble Falls Cemetery Association is a registered 501(c)(3) tax-exempt charitable organization founded on the principles of community, respect, and heritage. Our primary responsibility is the continued upkeep, beautification, and preservation of the historic Marble Falls Cemetery.

Because we operate as a non-profit, we rely entirely on the generosity of our community, the dedication of our volunteers, and the charitable donations of families who have loved ones resting here. Every dollar donated goes directly back into grounds maintenance, road repairs, tree trimming, and historical preservation efforts.

We believe that a well-maintained cemetery is a reflection of a community's love and respect for its founders and family members. We invite you to explore our grounds, participate in our clean-up days, and help us keep this sacred space beautiful for generations to come.

A Legacy of Marble Falls

For over a century, the Marble Falls Cemetery has served as the final resting place for the pioneers, veterans, and beloved citizens who built our wonderful city. Walking through the quiet avenues of the cemetery is akin to reading the history book of Texas itself.

The Association was formed by a group of concerned citizens who recognized the need for organized, perpetual care of the grounds. Before our 501(c)(3) incorporation, families were solely responsible for the upkeep of their individual family plots, which often led to neglected areas as families moved away or passed on.

Today, the Association bridges that gap. We meticulously map new interments, restore weathered headstones when possible, and ensure that even the oldest, forgotten graves are treated with the utmost dignity and respect.

Association Policies & Rules

To maintain the beauty, safety, and solemnity of the grounds, as well as comply with our non-profit governance, the Association strictly enforces the following policies.

Cemetery Grounds Rules

Visiting Hours: The cemetery is open to the public from dawn until dusk. No nighttime visitors are permitted.

Floral Arrangements: Fresh flowers are always welcome. Artificial flowers may be placed in approved vases. Glass containers, loose stones, and solar lights stuck directly into the turf are prohibited as they pose a hazard to our mowing equipment.

Clean-up Dates: The Association conducts grounds-wide clean-ups biannually (Spring and Fall). All weathered or non-compliant decorations will be respectfully removed during this time.

Privacy Policy

As a 501(c)(3) organization, we take your privacy seriously. We collect personal information (such as name, address, and email) solely for the purposes of processing donations, managing plot ownership records, and sending official Association correspondence.

We will never sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties. Online donations are processed through secure, encrypted third-party payment gateways, and we do not store your credit card information on our servers.
