About Our Non-Profit Association

The **Marble Falls Cemetery Association** is a dedicated 501(c)(3) tax-exempt non-profit organization. We operate solely for the purposeful maintenance and preservation of the historic Marble Falls Cemetery. Our association's primary goal is to ensure that these sacred grounds remain a peaceful, dignified, and visually appealing final resting place.

Because we are a non-profit, we do not receive government funding. We rely entirely on the generosity and kindness of our community. Charitable donations and plot purchases are vital to funding our continuous efforts.

Every donation received contributes directly to our work, enabling us to cover essential maintenance and essential landscape preservation.

A Living Legacy

The Marble Falls Cemetery holds more than just gravestones; it holds the rich history and unique stories of the pioneers, visionaries, and beloved families who have shaped our city.

Our association was established to organize the perpetual care and oversight of the cemetery. Initially, care was managed individually, but it became clear that a collective, long-term commitment was necessary.

Today, we carry that responsibility forward, meticulously updating records, preserving weathered headstones, and ensuring every single grave is respected for the generation it represents.

Our Enduring Mission

/* Replace with an image representing care, maintenance, or planting */

Our mission is simple yet profound: to maintain and protect the historic cemetery grounds in a manner that honors the memories of those interred. We strive to create a peaceful environment that provides comfort to visitors and is a source of civic pride for all of Marble Falls.

Our work includes regular landscaping, infrastructure repair, historical preservation, and the management of burial records and plot sales.

We believe that a well-cared-for cemetery speaks volumes about a community's values. By looking after this unique, quiet space, we are fulfilling our commitment to both the past and the future.

Association Rules & Conduct Policies

To ensure the safety, perpetual upkeep, and appropriate atmosphere, the following policies have been carefully considered and implemented.

Cemetery Grounds Policies

Visiting Hours: Access to the cemetery is permitted daily from dawn to dusk.

Flower and Decoration Policies: Fresh and artificial flowers are allowed within approved containers. Solar lights, glass vases, loose stones, and direct-in-ground decorations are strictly prohibited as they present significant hazards for maintenance operations.

General Clean-up: The Association performs twice-annual comprehensive grounds clean-ups. During these periods, all weathered, faded, or non-compliant decorations will be respectfully removed to prepare for seasonal growth and ensure proper appearance.

Privacy Policy

Your privacy is important to us. As a 501(c)(3), we collect and use specific personal information only as needed to manage plot ownership, process donations, and send official communication. We do not share or sell your personal details.

Online Security: All financial transactions made through our website are secured using industry-standard encryption, and your payment details are not stored by the Association.

Terms of Use

This website provides public information on cemetery maps, records, and policies. While we make every attempt to ensure information accuracy, the content is provided 'as is' and should be verified for historical research.

Ownership of a plot grants an easement for burial, not ownership of the underlying land, which remains the property and responsibility of the Association.
