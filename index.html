About The Association

The Marble Falls Cemetery Association is dedicated to the ongoing care, maintenance, and historical preservation of our beloved community cemetery. We provide a peaceful and respectful environment where families can remember and honor their loved ones.

Official 501(c)(3) Status: We are a federally recognized 501(c)(3) non-profit organization. This means that our operations are strictly driven by our mission rather than profit. It also means that all monetary and property donations made to the Marble Falls Cemetery Association are fully tax-deductible to the extent permitted by law.

Our volunteer board of directors works tirelessly to manage landscaping, road maintenance within the grounds, record-keeping, and the preservation of historical monuments dating back to the foundation of Marble Falls.

Our Rich History

Located deep in the Texas Hill Country, Marble Falls has a history defined by resilience, community, and pioneering spirit. The Marble Falls Cemetery has served as the final resting place for the founders, veterans, and generations of families who built this town.

Walking through the grounds is a walk through Texas history. From the early settlers of the 1800s to the heroes of the World Wars, the stones here tell the silent stories of the people of Burnet County.

The Association was formed by concerned citizens who realized that preserving this sacred ground required organized, communal effort. Today, we continue that legacy, ensuring that the stories of our ancestors are never lost to time or neglect.

Rules, Privacy & Policies

To maintain the beauty, dignity, and safety of the cemetery, and to protect the privacy of our patrons and donors, the Marble Falls Cemetery Association enforces the following policies.

Cemetery Rules & Regulations

To ensure a serene environment for all visitors:

  • Hours: The cemetery is open to the public from dawn to dusk daily.
  • Decorations: Artificial flowers are permitted but must be in an approved, secure vase. Glass containers, loose stones, and solar lights are strictly prohibited due to mowing hazards.
  • Planting: The planting of trees, shrubs, or perennial flowers on individual grave spaces is not permitted without board approval.
  • Pets: All pets must be kept on a leash, and owners must clean up after them.

Terms of Service

By using our website and services, you agree to the following terms:

  • Plot Purchases: Purchasing the right of interment does not convey ownership of the real estate, but rather the right to burial according to Association rules.
  • Liability: The Association is not liable for damage to headstones, monuments, or decorations caused by weather, vandalism, or natural deterioration.
  • Information Accuracy: While we strive to keep historical records accurate, we cannot guarantee the absolute precision of genealogical data provided on this site.

Privacy Policy

Your privacy is important to us as a non-profit organization.

  • Data Collection: We collect personal information (name, address, email) only when voluntarily submitted via our contact forms or donation portals.
  • Data Usage: Your information is used strictly to process donations, issue tax receipts, and communicate official Association business.
  • Third Parties: We will never sell, trade, or rent your personal identification information to others.
  • Security: We adopt appropriate data collection and processing practices to protect against unauthorized access to your personal information.
